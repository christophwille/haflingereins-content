JohnBennett replied on 2020-04-06 08:54

On the wiper motor where the wires are connected, there is a micro switch in the back plastic part. Take the cover off and look at how it works.

There is a cam shape on the rotating part of the motor which pushes a pin through a hole. The pin either opens or closes the micro switch.

Power comes in on one wire, another wire also has power. The third wire is ground. When the wiper switch is on, the power comes along two line. When the power is switch off, the power comes via the micro switch. When the motor turns one complete revolution, the cam pushes the pin which breaks the power line so the motor stops. If the motor has only just passed the point at which it pushes the pin, then there is still power to make the motor turn.
