JohnBennett replied on 2021-09-19 10:11

Nothing to do with the clutch unless it is slipping - i.e. the engine RPM goes higher but the road speed does not increase.

Nothing to do with the transmission if you can select all the gears unless it is the brakes binding - do the wheels drums get hot if you just drive - I.e. not putting the brakes on to stop?

Most likely reasons are the fuel mixture is not right or the ignition timing is not right.

JohnBennett replied on 2021-09-19 22:40

Do you mean you now have an electric fuel pump? If so does it provide fuel at the correct pressure? 2 or 3 psi is more than enough, too much and you will be flooding the carb and the engine will be running rich. Not good for performance or fuel economy. Too little and it will be starving the carb and performance it suffer. Does it run better with the choke out or in? If in, maybe too much pressure. If out, then too little pressure - or at least fuel flow.

You could check your fuel filter. Even if it is new, it might have filled up with rubbish and be restricting the fuel flow.

Also check the ignition timing.
